J. Robert Oppenheimer

1904 - 1967

"Father of the Atomic Bomb"

The most well-known portrait of Oppenheimer.

Julius Robert Oppenheimer (April 22, 1904 – February 18, 1967) was an American theoretical physicist and professor of physics. He is best known as the scientific director of the Manhattan Project, the World War II project that developed the first nuclear weapons.

Timeline of Oppenheimer's Life

  • 1904 - Born in New York City, USA.
  • 1925 - Graduated from Harvard University.
  • 1927 - Earned PhD in physics from the University of Göttingen, Germany.
  • 1929 - Began teaching at the University of California, Berkeley.
  • 1942 - Appointed scientific director of the Manhattan Project.
  • 1945 - Witnessed the Trinity Test, the first detonation of a nuclear weapon.
  • 1945 - Hiroshima and Nagasaki bombings brought WWII to an end.
  • 1947 - Became director of the Institute for Advanced Study at Princeton.
  • 1954 - Security clearance revoked during the Red Scare hearings.
  • 1963 - Received the Enrico Fermi Award.
  • 1967 - Died in Princeton, New Jersey, at age 62.
